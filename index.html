Nishka Katoch

I am a machine learning researcher with a Master's in Computer Science from The University of Montreal and Mila, and currently an intern at Tech Mahindra Maker's Lab. My work spans computer vision, generative AI, and domain adaptation, with a focus on building privacy-preserving and adaptable AI systems for real-world settings.

At the University of Montreal and Mila, I pursued my Master's as part of the NeuroPoly Lab at Polytechnique Montréal under the supervision of Prof. Julien Cohen-Adad and Prof. Guy Wolf, where my thesis explored multi-contrast image translation for axon and myelin segmentation. This work used diffusion-based frameworks to generate privacy-preserving synthetic microscopy images. During this time, I also contributed to the lab’s open-source projects, ivadomed, a deep learning toolbox for medical image segmentation, and AxonDeepSeg, a framework for axon and myelin segmentation from microscopy images.

Prior to my current role, I gained research and industry experience through several internships and collaborations. At Custom Quality Build, I designed computer vision pipelines that combined diffusion models with 3D rendering to enable interactive home design visualization. Earlier, as a research intern at Tech Mahindra Maker's Lab, I worked on automatic speech recognition for Hindi and other Indic languages, fine-tuning transformer models to improve multilingual speech recognition. I also interned at the Centre for Railway Information Systems, where I developed scalable Django applications and streamlined data pipelines for operational efficiency as part of the BLW Indian Railways project. During my undergraduate studies, I explored robotics and human-computer interaction under Prof. P.B. Sujit at IIIT Delhi, where I built VR-based UAV swarm simulations, and later carried out an independent research study on scaling laws in self-supervised speech models. These experiences gave me a strong foundation across applied AI systems, from speech and vision to large-scale deployment.

Looking ahead, I am especially drawn to:

  • Advancing computer vision and Generative AI: Developing models that can interpret, reason about, and manipulate visual data, including images, video, and multimodal inputs.
  • Multimodal and agentic AI: Building AI systems that integrate diverse modalities and act autonomously to achieve goals, enabling planning, decision-making, and collaboration in complex environments.
